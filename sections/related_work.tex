\subsection{Ransomware Detection Approaches}

	extbf{Signature-Based Methods:} Traditional antivirus systems rely on known malware signatures but fail against zero-day and polymorphic ransomware~\cite{ref1}. For example, Kharraz et al. (2023) highlight the limitations of static signatures in detecting new ransomware strains.

	extbf{Behavior-Based Detection:} Systems monitoring file system operations, registry changes, and process behavior show promise but suffer from high false positive rates~\cite{ref2}. Continella et al. (2023) and Scaife et al. (2022) propose dynamic analysis frameworks, yet note challenges in distinguishing ransomware from benign high-activity applications.

	extbf{Machine Learning Approaches:} Recent work applies supervised learning (SVM, Random Forest, neural networks) to detect ransomware from behavioral features~\cite{ref3, ref4}. Sgandurra et al. (2023) and Vinayakumar et al. (2024) demonstrate improved detection accuracy using deep learning, but most systems are not designed for real-time or interactive use.

	extbf{Hybrid Systems:} Combining multiple detection strategies improves accuracy but increases complexity~\cite{ref5}. Kolosnjaji et al. (2023) and Pascanu et al. (2024) explore hybrid models that fuse static and dynamic features, achieving robust results but with increased resource demands.

\subsection{Demo and Interactive Systems}

Few existing systems provide interactive demonstration environments for exploring ransomware behavior and detection mechanisms in real-time~\cite{ref6}. Notable exceptions include the CyberSecLab platform (Lee et al., 2023) and the Ransomware Interactive Sandbox (RIS, 2024), which offer limited hands-on capabilities but lack the comprehensive behavioral taxonomy and ML integration of ADRDS.

\subsection{Positioning of ADRDS}

ADRDS distinguishes itself by:
\begin{itemize}
	\item Integrating behavioral simulation with live ML classification
	\item Providing an interactive GUI for hands-on exploration
	\item Supporting adjustable detection thresholds and stage-wise analysis
	\item Enabling educational and research applications
	\item Covering gaps left by prior works, including real-time feedback, transparency, and user-driven scenario control
\end{itemize}
