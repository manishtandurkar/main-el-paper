\section{Discussion}

Following the examination of the architecture, experiments, and results, this section reflects on the efficacy of the proposed approach, acknowledges limitations, and outlines future research directions.

\subsection{Significance of Behavior-Based Detection}

The results substantiate the premise that behavioral monitoring offers superior efficacy compared to static analysis. By organizing detection around a stage-aware behavioral taxonomy, ADRDS identifies ransomware during early reconnaissance and file enumeration phases, potentially prior to the initiation of encryption. This proactive detection capability is critical for mitigating data loss.

\subsection{Efficacy of Hybrid Models}

The combination of supervised classifiers with an unsupervised anomaly detector demonstrated clear advantages. Supervised learning effectively identifies known attack patterns, while the autoencoder detects novel variants. Risk fusion integrates these strengths, reducing false positives without compromising sensitivity. This robustness exceeds that of either approach in isolation.

\subsection{Transparency and Interactivity}

Feedback from demonstrations highlights the value of interpretability. Correlation of detection outputs with specific lifecycle stages and real-time risk scoring transforms ADRDS from an opaque system into an educational tool. Security analysts can trace alerts to behavioral origins, while students can experimentally verify the impact of threshold adjustments.

\subsection{Operational Feasibility}

Sub-second end-to-end latency ensures that ADRDS remains practical for interactive applications. Whether utilized in academic exercises or for exploring edge-case scenarios, the system provides immediate feedback, facilitating natural and efficient exploration.

\subsection{Limitations}

It is necessary to acknowledge current limitations. First, experiments utilized simulated behavior, which may not capture the full complexity of real-world malware. Second, the system relies on host-level telemetry, which may not be available in all environments. Third, threshold tuning remains context-dependent, requiring adjustment for specific deployment scenarios.

\subsection{Future Work}

Several extensions are proposed for future development. The incorporation of system call traces and network traffic analysis could enhance detection fidelity. Experimentation with transformer-based or graph-based models may enable the capture of more complex behavioral relationships. Furthermore, integrating explainable AI (XAI) techniques would further improve transparency and user trust.

In conclusion, ADRDS serves as a foundational platform. The modular design and open exploration philosophy are intended to encourage further research, advancing ransomware detection while maintaining a focus on safety and interpretability.
