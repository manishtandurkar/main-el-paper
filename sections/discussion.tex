\subsection{Key Insights}

\textbf{Behavioral Features Matter:} The strong performance of ML models validates 
our behavioral taxonomy approach. Features related to file entropy changes and 
high-frequency file operations proved most discriminative.

\textbf{Stage-Based Detection:} Early-stage detection capabilities demonstrate 
the value of comprehensive behavioral modeling beyond just encryption-phase monitoring.

\textbf{Model Trade-offs:} Random Forest's superior performance stems from its 
ability to capture non-linear feature interactions without overfitting, unlike 
neural networks which require larger training sets.

\subsection{Limitations}

\textbf{Dataset Constraints:} Our evaluation uses simulated and lab-environment 
data. Real-world deployment may encounter behavioral variations not captured in 
training data.

\textbf{Evasion Potential:} Sophisticated attackers aware of our detection features 
could potentially craft evasive behaviors (e.g., rate-limiting file operations). 
Adversarial robustness requires ongoing research.

\textbf{Zero-Day Detection:} While our approach generalizes better than signature-based 
methods, novel ransomware families with radically different behaviors may evade 
detection until retraining.

\textbf{False Positives:} Legitimate applications with aggressive file operations 
(e.g., backup software, video editing) may trigger false alerts. Production deployment 
requires careful threshold tuning and whitelisting.
