\section{Results and Evaluation}
\label{sec:results}

In this section, we present the empirical evidence supporting the efficacy of ADRDS. We analyze the detection accuracy across different classifiers, evaluate the trade-off between sensitivity and specificity using ROC curves, and assess the system's operational suitability through latency measurements.

\subsection{Detection Performance}

Table~\ref{tab:results} presents the comparative performance metrics for the evaluated models. We included a signature-based baseline (ClamAV) to highlight the gap between static and dynamic detection methods.

% TABLE 2: CLASSIFICATION RESULTS
\begin{table}[htbp]
\caption{Classification Performance Comparison}
\label{tab:results}
\centering
\begin{tabular}{|l|c|c|c|c|c|}
\hline
\textbf{Model} & \textbf{Acc} & \textbf{Prec} & \textbf{Rec} & \textbf{F1} & \textbf{AUC} \\
\hline
\textbf{Random Forest} & \textbf{98.5\%} & \textbf{98.2\%} & \textbf{98.8\%} & \textbf{98.5\%} & \textbf{0.99} \\
SVM & 92.4\% & 91.0\% & 93.1\% & 92.0\% & 0.94 \\
Neural Network & 95.8\% & 94.5\% & 96.2\% & 95.3\% & 0.97 \\
Baseline (Sig.) & 45.0\% & 100\% & 45.0\% & 62.0\% & 0.50 \\
\hline
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

\textbf{Key Findings:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Superiority of Random Forest:} The Random Forest classifier achieves the highest overall accuracy of 98.5\% with a balanced profile between Precision and Recall. Its ensemble nature effectively reduces variance, making it highly robust against the noisy system logs typical of the training environment.
    
    \item \textbf{Failure of Static Baselines:} As expected, the signature-based baseline performed poorly (45\% accuracy) against our dataset of polymorphic variants. While it had perfect precision (no false positives), it completely failed to detect new, unseen ransomware strains, validating the necessity of our behavioral approach.
    
    \item \textbf{Low False Positive Rate:} Across all ML models, the False Positive Rate (FPR) remained below 2.0\%. This is a critical metric for production deployment, ensuring that security analysts are not overwhelmed by alert fatigue from benign high-load applications.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Stage-Wise Detection}

By correlating detection timestamps with our L1 Behavioral Taxonomy, we analyzed \textit{when} the system catches the attack:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{65\% detected during Reconnaissance:} The majority of attacks were flagged during the initial file traversal and directory enumeration phase, preventing encryption entirely.
    
    \item \textbf{25\% detected at Encryption Start:} These attacks were caught immediately upon the first burst of \texttt{CryptEncrypt} calls, resulting in zero or negligible data loss.
    
    \item \textbf{10\% detected within first 5 files:} The remaining "stealthy" variants were detected after encrypting fewer than 5 files, as the entropy statistics rapidly converged to the malicious threshold.
\end{itemize}

This distribution proves that ADRDS successfully shifts the security posture from reactive recovery to proactive mitigation.
