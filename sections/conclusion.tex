\section{Conclusion}

This paper presented the AI-Driven Ransomware Detection and Response Simulator (ADRDS), a modular and interactive framework designed to address the limitations of traditional signature-based ransomware detection systems. By adopting a behavior-centric perspective, ADRDS models ransomware activity through a structured lifecycle taxonomy and leverages a hybrid machine learning approach that combines supervised classification with unsupervised anomaly detection.

The proposed system demonstrates that integrating behavioral simulation, real-time feature extraction, and hybrid detection enables early and robust identification of ransomware activity, including previously unseen attack variants. The inclusion of an interactive visualization layer further enhances transparency and interpretability, allowing users to observe attack progression, understand detection decisions, and explore detection trade-offs in real time.

Experimental evaluation shows that ADRDS achieves high detection accuracy with low latency, making it well suited for interactive demonstrations, educational use, and exploratory security research. By emphasizing safety, explainability, and user-driven experimentation, ADRDS provides a practical platform for advancing ransomware detection research and fostering deeper understanding of modern ransomware behavior.
